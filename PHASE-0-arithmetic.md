# Phase 0 — Arithmetic Fluency

**Duration:** 2–4 weeks. Strictly 10–15 min/day. Do not exceed; this is a daily-drip habit, not a weekend project.

**Goal:** Restore the mental-math confidence you had at 17. By the end you can:
- Add or subtract 3-digit numbers in your head, in under 5 seconds.
- Multiply 2-digit × 2-digit in your head, under 5 seconds, > 90% accuracy.
- Multiply 3-digit × 2-digit on paper or in your head, under 20 seconds.
- Estimate any real-world quantity to within an order of magnitude in 30 seconds (Fermi).

---

## Why this phase exists

Math fear isn't intellectual — it's somatic. You feel cold when someone asks "what's 23 × 14?" because your nervous system has learned that arithmetic = possible humiliation. The fix is not to "understand more math." The fix is reps. Daily, low-stakes, no-judgment reps. You will be shocked how fast it returns.

## Week-by-week

### Week 1 — Re-establish the floor

**Daily, ~10 min:**
- 5 min: 30 random 2-digit additions and subtractions. Aim for accuracy, then speed.
- 5 min: 1-digit multiplication table review (1×1 through 12×12). Should be instant. If not, drill the gaps until they are.

**Technique focus: left-to-right arithmetic.**
- 47 + 38 = (40+30) + (7+8) = 70 + 15 = 85. Do it left to right, the way you say numbers.
- 234 + 567 = 700 + 90 + 11 = 801.
- This is faster *and* less error-prone than the school right-to-left method, because partial sums are useful even if you stop early (good for estimation).

**End-of-week checkpoint:** 30 mixed 2-digit additions in 90 seconds, > 95% accuracy.

### Week 2 — Cross-multiplication (Vedic Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam)

This is the trick that gets you 2×2 multiplication in under 5 seconds.

**For 23 × 41:**
```
   2 3
 × 4 1
 ─────
```
- **Right:** 3 × 1 = 3 → ones digit.
- **Cross:** 2×1 + 3×4 = 2 + 12 = 14 → tens digit, carry 1.
- **Left:** 2 × 4 = 8, plus carry 1 = 9 → hundreds digit.
- Answer: **943**.

You'll be slow for the first three days. By day 5 it clicks. By day 10 it's automatic.

**Daily, ~10 min:**
- 5 min: 20 random 2×2 multiplications using cross-multiplication.
- 5 min: 10 random 2×1 multiplications, as warm-up.

**End-of-week checkpoint:** 20 random 2×2 multiplications in 3 minutes, > 90% accuracy.

### Week 3 — Squaring tricks and divisibility

**Squaring numbers ending in 5:** *N5² = N(N+1) followed by 25.*
- 35² = 3·4 | 25 = 1225.
- 75² = 7·8 | 25 = 5625.

**Squaring near 100:** *(100-a)² = (100 - 2a) followed by a².*
- 97² = 94 | 09 = 9409 (a = 3, a² = 09).
- 92² = 84 | 64 = 8464.

**Squaring near 50:** *(50+a)² = (25+a) | a²* and *(50-a)² = (25-a) | a²*.
- 53² = 28 | 09 = 2809.

**Divisibility tests** (Anki these):
- by 3 or 9: sum of digits divisible by 3 or 9.
- by 11: alternating sum of digits divisible by 11.
- by 7: take last digit, double it, subtract from rest, repeat. (Or just learn modular arithmetic.)

**Daily, ~10 min:** Practice one technique per day. Pen-and-paper.

### Week 4 — Estimation and Fermi problems

Mental math is not just for grocery bills — it's for **bullshit detection**. When someone quotes a statistic, you should be able to sanity-check it in your head.

**Daily exercise: One Fermi problem a day.** Examples:
- How many cups of coffee are drunk worldwide in a day?
- How much does the air in a typical living room weigh?
- How many words have you spoken in your life?
- How many neurons fire in your brain in 1 second?

Method:
1. Decompose into multiplied factors you can estimate (population × frequency × ...).
2. Round aggressively (powers of 10 are fine).
3. Multiply.
4. Look up the answer. Be within an order of magnitude.

This is the single most professionally useful arithmetic skill there is.

## Exit criteria for Phase 0

You can do all of these without sweating:
- [ ] 2-digit + 2-digit addition: < 3 seconds, in your head.
- [ ] 2-digit × 2-digit multiplication: < 5 seconds, in your head, > 90% accurate.
- [ ] 3-digit × 2-digit on paper: < 20 seconds, > 90% accurate.
- [ ] Square any 2-digit number using one of the tricks: < 8 seconds.
- [ ] Estimate a Fermi problem to within an order of magnitude: < 60 seconds.
- [ ] The phrase "what's X times Y" does not produce a fear response.

When all six are checked, move to [Phase 1](PHASE-1-foundations.md).

But — and this is important — keep doing the 10-min daily drill *forever*. Arithmetic fluency atrophies if you stop. It's like running.

---

## On feeling stupid

You will, in the first three days, feel slow and embarrassed even though no one is watching. That's normal. It is also temporary. By the end of week 1 you will already feel sharper. By the end of week 4 you will feel like you got your brain back. Trust the process.
