How this works

Most people who try to "get good at math again" pick up a textbook, study heroically for two weekends, then quit. This plan is the opposite: short daily lessons, in your browser, with measurable monthly skill gains so you stay motivated.

10 min / day · forever

Track A — Fluency drill

Mental arithmetic, every day. This kills the "fear of math" layer in week 1 and prevents it from coming back. Open the drill →

30–35 min / day

Track B — Today's lesson

One interactive lesson per day, ordered to build on the previous. Hands-on widget + auto-graded exercises. ~180 lessons over 6 months. Open the hub →

Your daily routine

  1. 10 min — Track A: open the arithmetic drill, do 30 problems. Difficulty 2-digit × 2-digit by week 2.
  2. 30 min — Track B: open the hub, click today's lesson, do it end-to-end.
  3. 30 seconds: click "mark today complete" at the bottom of the lesson. Your streak persists in the browser.

The unbreakable rule: never skip two days in a row. One missed day is recoverable; two starts breaking the habit. If you genuinely can't do the full 45 min, do JUST the 10-min arithmetic drill — that alone keeps the streak.

How to start today

  1. Open the arithmetic drill. Do 30 problems at 2-digit difficulty. Don't worry about speed yet — accuracy first.
  2. Open the matrix transformation lesson: matrices as transformations. Play with it for 15 minutes. Try every preset. Type in random matrices.
  3. Tomorrow, the Day 1 Fermi lesson will be built. We'll iterate week by week — that way each batch can be informed by what you found easy or hard.

Why we build week-by-week instead of all at once: I don't yet know which topics will be easy for you and which will be sticky. Building one week at a time lets us adjust depth, pace, and emphasis based on your actual signals — which exercises felt too easy, which made you stop and think, which topics need extra reinforcement. Pre-committing to all 180 lessons up front would lock in guesses.
