Honest calibration on Google paths

Different roles need different things. The math curriculum optimizes for the SWE + ML-Eng intersection.

PathRealistic timelineWhat the math gets you
SWE L4 6–18 months Math is light here; algorithms + Big-O + discrete math matter most. Project Euler practice IS the math practice for this path.
ML Engineer 12–24 months + portfolio This is where the math plan helps most directly. Linear algebra, calc, prob/stats, optimization, PyTorch fluency.
Research (Brain/DeepMind) Multi-year — usually needs PhD or published work Math is necessary but not sufficient. To go this route, plan to publish (workshops count) or replicate SOTA papers publicly. The math journey is your foundation; the publications are the proof.
If Research is the real goal: in parallel with this curriculum, plan to (a) replicate one published ML paper end-to-end every 2–3 months starting Month 4, (b) start a technical blog by Month 4, (c) by Month 12 begin contributing to an open-source ML project. Math alone won't get you in; output will.

Your daily routine

  1. 10 min — Track A: arithmetic drill. 30 problems.
  2. 30 min — Tracks B+C: open the hub, do today's lesson.
  3. 30 sec: click "mark today complete" at the bottom of the lesson.
  4. Saturday: add 60 min for Track D (side project work).
The unbreakable rule: never skip two days in a row. If you can only do 10 min, do JUST the arithmetic drill — that alone keeps the streak.
