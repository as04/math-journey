What the matrix is telling you

quantity value geometric meaning
det(M) 1 signed area-scaling factor. Negative = orientation flipped. Zero = collapses to a line.
trace(M) = a + d 2 sum of eigenvalues.
eigenvalues 1, 1 scaling factors along the special "eigen-directions."
eigenvectors any directions that don't change direction under M (drawn as dashed yellow lines if real).

The intuitions to lock in

  1. A matrix takes î = (1,0) to the first column, and ĵ = (0,1) to the second column. Everything else is determined by linearity.
  2. det(M) is how much area scales. Type [[2,0],[0,3]] and watch the unit square become a 2×3 rectangle, area 6. Type [[1,1],[0,1]] (shear) — area stays 1.
  3. det(M) = 0 means the transformation crushes the plane onto a line. Try [[1,2],[2,4]]. There is no inverse — you can't undo a crush.
  4. Eigenvectors are the directions where the transformation acts like a simple stretch. Try [[2,0],[0,0.5]] — î stretches by 2, ĵ shrinks by 0.5. Both are eigenvectors.
  5. A rotation has complex eigenvalues — there's no real direction that's unchanged. Try rotate 30° — eigenvalues complex, no real eigen-lines.
Self-test: Before computing, predict the determinant of [[1,2],[3,4]]. Then type it in. Was your sign right? (Answer below.) Determinant = 1·4 − 2·3 = −2. Negative → orientation flipped.
