30 days in. Pause and look back.

If you did all 30 days, here's what changed:

  • Arithmetic. The fear is gone. 2-digit × 2-digit is fast. Fermi estimates are second nature.
  • Linear algebra. You see matrices as transformations. Eigenvectors as fixed directions. Span and basis as geometric objects.
  • Probability + stats. Bayes is a tool you reach for. Variance and covariance are not just formulas. Sample spaces are real to you.
  • Calculus. Derivative-as-rate is internalized. Chain rule is the engine of backprop in your head.
  • Optimization. Gradient descent is no longer mysterious.
  • ML. You've implemented linear regression from scratch — twice (closed form + GD). It's in your public GitHub.
  • Algorithms. Big-O fluency. Sorting tradeoffs. Recursion + first DP. Hashing pattern recognition.
  • Project Euler. 3+ problems solved with both brute force and math.

This is real. Acknowledge it before moving on.
