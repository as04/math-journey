The honest check

You've worked through 28 lessons. Today: pick up a blank piece of paper and re-derive five key results from memory, no notes. If you can, the knowledge is yours. If you can't on some, that tells you exactly what to revisit before moving to Month 2.

This is the highest-return activity in the entire curriculum. Re-derivation reveals what you've actually internalized vs. what just felt familiar. Most people are shocked the first time they try this — and rebuilt by the third.

The five re-derivations

1. Chain rule

From the limit definition h'(x) = lim (h(x+δ) − h(x))/δ, derive d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x).

Hint: multiply numerator and denominator by (g(x+δ) − g(x)). Day 21.

2. Bayes' theorem

From the definition of conditional probability, derive P(A|B) = P(B|A) · P(A) / P(B).

Hint: two ways of writing P(A ∩ B). Day 10.

3. Normal equations for linear regression

Starting from L(β) = (1/n) · ‖y − Xβ‖², derive β* = (XᵀX)⁻¹Xᵀy.

Hint: expand the squared norm, take gradient w.r.t. β, set to zero. Day 24.

4. Variance from two algebraic forms

Show that Var(X) = E[(X−μ)²] = E[X²] − (E[X])².

Hint: expand the square, use linearity of expectation, recognize E[X] = μ. Day 12.

5. Dot product = |u||v|cosθ

For vectors u, v in ℝ², show that u·v = u₁v₁ + u₂v₂ = |u| · |v| · cos(θ) where θ is the angle between them.

Hint: write u and v in polar form (|u|cosα, |u|sinα), use angle-difference identity for cosine. Day 3.

The process — strict

  1. For each result, give yourself 15 minutes. No notes. No lookups.
  2. If you can write it cleanly: nailed it.
  3. If you get stuck in the middle: write down where you got stuck. That's the gap.
  4. Only after attempting all 5, look up the proofs from the relevant day's lesson.
  5. Whatever was hardest, that's your priority for the first week of Month 2 — re-do that lesson's exercises slowly.
