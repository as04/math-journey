Why this is foundational for ML

Every data point in ML is a vector. Every neuron's input is a vector. Every embedding (word, image, user, song) is a vector. The arithmetic of vectors — addition, scalar multiplication, dot product — is the primitive that everything else is built on. Get the geometric mental model now and the rest of ML math falls into place.

The mental model

  • A vector is an arrow from the origin to a point.
  • Its magnitude is its length. For v = (a, b): |v| = √(a² + b²). (Pythagoras.)
  • Addition: place arrows tip-to-tail. Geometrically: the resultant from the first origin to the last tip.
  • Scalar multiplication: stretch the arrow by that factor (negative flips it).
  • Dot product: u · v = u₁v₁ + u₂v₂ = |u||v| cos θ. The "amount of u that's pointing along v" — a similarity measure.
The dot product is the most important formula in ML you don't yet appreciate. Cosine similarity is just normalized dot product. Attention is dot products of query and key. Word embedding similarity is dot product. Linear regression prediction is dot product of features and weights. Internalize it now.
