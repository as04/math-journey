Calculus, restarted as an adult

School calculus felt mechanical because it was taught mechanically. Today is the reframe: a derivative is a rate of change. Period. Every formula, every rule, every trick is just a way to compute that one thing.

And we're building toward gradient descent in 3 days. Understanding rate-of-change is the prerequisite for understanding why ML training works at all.

The mental model

Take a function f. Pick a point x. Ask: "if I nudge x by a tiny amount h, how much does f(x) change?"

The average rate of change over an interval of width h is:

(f(x + h) − f(x)) / h

The derivative (instantaneous rate of change) is what this approaches as h → 0:

f'(x) = lim_{h→0} (f(x + h) − f(x)) / h

That ratio is the slope of the line through (x, f(x)) and (x+h, f(x+h)) — a secant. As h shrinks, the secant rotates toward the tangent line at x. The derivative is its slope.
