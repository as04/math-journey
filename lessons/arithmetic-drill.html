The cross-multiplication trick (Vedic Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam)

For two-digit × two-digit, e.g. 23 × 41:

  • Right column: 3 × 1 = 3 → ones digit.
  • Cross: 2×1 + 3×4 = 14 → tens digit, carry 1.
  • Left column: 2 × 4 = 8, plus carry 1 = 9 → hundreds digit.
  • Result: 943.

Slow for the first three days. Automatic by day ten. Trust it.

Squaring near 100 (or 50)

  • 97² = (100−3)² = (100−2·3) | 3² = 94 | 09 = 9409.
  • 53² = (50+3)² = (25+3) | 3² = 28 | 09 = 2809.

Left-to-right addition

For 347 + 286: do hundreds first, then tens, then ones — exactly how you say the number.
300+200 = 500, 40+80 = 120 → 620, 7+6 = 13 → 633.

Faster, fewer errors, and partial sums are useful for estimation.
